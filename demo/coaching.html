Coaching Tips

Set a team goal: A shared goal can unite the team and help keep everyone focused on success.

Keep players engaged: Rotate positions to keep players engaged and help them find their best position.

Be positive: Avoid negative reinforcement, which can demotivate players and lead to anxiety.

Get feedback: Ask players what they like and don't like about drills so you can improve.

Teach competition: Drills like the errors game can help players develop a winning attitude.

Be adaptable: Be open to change and ready to overcome challenges.

Make practice fun: Include lots of repetitions, and stop to point out when players do something well.

Give pre- and post-game talks: Keep these talks short and focused on a few main points.

Stress sportsmanship: Emphasize the importance of being a good sport.
